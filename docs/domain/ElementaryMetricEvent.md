# 1. Purpose

The purpose of the `ElementaryMetricEvent` (EME) entity is to represent the smallest observable temporal event generated by an active `MetricContributor`.

The EME constitutes the elementary unit from which the ensemble metric structure is progressively inferred.

It is the first temporal object of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

---

# 2. Scientific Motivation

The temporal organisation of an ensemble cannot be analysed directly from the continuous audio signal.

Instead, the signal must be decomposed into discrete temporal events representing observable musical actions that contribute to the collective metric reference.

The ElementaryMetricEvent provides this fundamental abstraction.

Every higher-level temporal structure within the JGA is ultimately derived from collections of EMEs.

---

# 3. Scientific Definition

An `ElementaryMetricEvent` is an observable temporal event generated by a `MetricContributor`.

An EME represents the occurrence of a metric-relevant musical action at a specific instant within the performance.

The EME is an observation.

It is not yet an estimation of pulse, beat or tempo.

# 5. Responsibilities

The ElementaryMetricEvent is responsible for:

- representing one metric-relevant temporal event;
- preserving its temporal position;
- maintaining traceability to the originating MetricContributor;
- serving as the elementary input for MetricCluster construction.

The EME never:

- estimates pulse;
- estimates tempo;
- belongs to a beat;
- performs statistical analysis.

---

# 6. Relationships

Every ElementaryMetricEvent originates from exactly one MetricContributor.

Multiple EMEs contribute to the construction of one MetricCluster.

Every MetricCluster is composed exclusively of EMEs.

---

# 7. Lifecycle

An EME is created when a metric-relevant event is detected.

Once created, it remains immutable throughout the complete analysis.

---

# 8. Domain Invariants

The following invariants shall always remain true.

## Invariant 1

Every EME possesses one and only one temporal position.

---

## Invariant 2

Every EME originates from exactly one MetricContributor.

---

## Invariant 3

An EME is an observation.

It never represents an estimated temporal reference.

---

## Invariant 4

An EME never belongs simultaneously to multiple MetricClusters.

---

## Invariant 5

The temporal position of an EME never changes.

---

# 9. Constraints

An ElementaryMetricEvent shall never:

- estimate pulse;
- estimate beat;
- estimate tempo;
- estimate the Internal Metric Timeline.

Those responsibilities belong to later stages of the Domain Model.

---

# 10. Observability

The ElementaryMetricEvent is directly derived from observable musical events.

It represents the smallest temporal entity explicitly modelled by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

# 12. Future Software Representation

Within the software architecture, the ElementaryMetricEvent will be implemented as an immutable Domain Entity.

Its implementation shall preserve every responsibility, invariant and relationship defined in this specification.

Implementation details remain outside the scope of this document.

---

# 13. Conclusion

The ElementaryMetricEvent constitutes the fundamental temporal atom of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

Every higher-level temporal object—including MetricCluster, Pulse and InternalMetricTimeline—is ultimately derived from collections of ElementaryMetricEvents.

For this reason, the EME represents the foundational temporal entity of the entire metric analysis process.
