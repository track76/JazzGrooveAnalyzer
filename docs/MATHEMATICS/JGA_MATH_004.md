# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to introduce the fundamental mathematical objects constituting the formal model of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

Each object defined herein represents one and only one theoretical concept previously established within the conceptual framework of the project.

The present document introduces the objects themselves.

Their mathematical properties and interactions are defined in subsequent documents.

---

# 2. Mathematical Objects

The Jazz Groove Analyzer is constructed upon a finite collection of fundamental mathematical objects.

Each object possesses:

- a unique definition;

- a unique mathematical meaning;

- a unique notation;

- a unique position within the abstraction hierarchy.

No object may represent more than one theoretical concept.

Likewise, no theoretical concept may be represented by multiple mathematical objects.

---

# 3. Observation Objects

Observation Objects represent directly observable phenomena extracted from the recorded audio signal.

They belong to the Observation Domain and do not contain musical interpretation.

The Observation Objects are:

• Audio Signal

• Transient

• Onset

• Pulse Candidate

These objects constitute the observational foundation of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

---

# 4. Representation Objects

Representation Objects belong to the Representation Domain.

They are mathematical abstractions describing progressively richer representations of musical time.

The Representation Objects are:

• Elementary Metric Event

• Metric Cluster

• Pulse

• Internal Metric Timeline

These objects are not directly observable within the physical signal.

They exist exclusively within the mathematical model.

---

# 5. Object Identity

Every mathematical object possesses its own identity.

Two objects remain mathematically distinct even if they refer to the same temporal position.

Consequently,

temporal coincidence

does not imply

mathematical identity.

---

# 6. Object Independence

Each mathematical object is independent from every computational implementation.

Its existence depends exclusively upon the formal mathematical model.

Algorithms operate on mathematical objects.

They never define them.

---

# 7. Object Hierarchy

The mathematical objects of the Jazz Groove Analyzer are organized according to the abstraction hierarchy previously established by the theoretical framework.

Observation Domain

↓

Representation Domain

Every object occupies exactly one level of abstraction.

No object may simultaneously belong to multiple abstraction levels.

---

# 8. Object Stability

Once introduced into the mathematical framework, an object preserves its mathematical meaning.

Future extensions of the Jazz Groove Analyzer may introduce additional objects.

However, previously defined objects shall never change their formal meaning.

This guarantees the long-term stability of the mathematical framework.

---

# 9. Fundamental Principles

Every mathematical object satisfies the following principles.

## Principle 1

Uniqueness.

Every object represents exactly one theoretical concept.

---

## Principle 2

Identity.

Every object possesses its own mathematical identity.

---

## Principle 3

---

## Principle 4
