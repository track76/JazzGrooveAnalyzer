---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the mathematical structures adopted by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

While the previous document introduces the fundamental mathematical objects, the present document specifies how such objects are organized within the formal mathematical framework.

The structures introduced herein are independent of computational implementation and constitute the formal organization of the mathematical model.

---

# 2. Mathematical Structure

A mathematical structure is an organized collection of mathematical objects together with the rules governing their organization.

Within the Jazz Groove Analyzer, mathematical structures provide the formal framework required to represent progressively richer descriptions of musical time.

Mathematical structures never define computational procedures.

They define only the formal organization of mathematical objects.

---

# 3. Sets

Mathematical objects belonging to the same conceptual category are organized into sets.

A set contains only homogeneous objects.

Consequently,

- Observation Objects belong to observation sets;

- Representation Objects belong to representation sets.

No set may simultaneously contain objects belonging to different abstraction levels.

---

# 4. Ordered Structures

Whenever temporal order is meaningful, mathematical objects are organized into ordered structures.

Ordering preserves the chronological organization of the represented phenomena.

Temporal ordering is independent of musical interpretation.

It reflects only the formal organization of mathematical objects.

---

# 5. Collections

Some mathematical structures are collections.

A collection groups several mathematical objects while preserving the identity of every individual element.

Membership in a collection never modifies the mathematical properties of its elements.

The Metric Cluster represents the canonical example of this structural principle.

---

# 6. Hierarchical Structures

The mathematical framework of the Jazz Groove Analyzer is hierarchical.

Higher-level structures are constructed upon lower-level structures.

No hierarchical level replaces the previous one.

Instead, each level extends the mathematical description provided by the preceding level.

---

# 7. Structural Coherence

Every mathematical structure satisfies the following conditions.

- internal consistency;

- unique mathematical interpretation;

- compatibility with the abstraction hierarchy;

- independence from implementation.

These conditions guarantee the coherence of the complete mathematical framework.

---

# 8. Structural Independence

The mathematical structures defined by the Jazz Groove Analyzer are abstract entities.

They do not prescribe software data structures, programming paradigms or implementation strategies.

Different computational implementations may realize the same mathematical structure without modifying its formal definition.

---

# 9. Fundamental Principles

Every mathematical structure satisfies the following principles.

## Principle 1

Homogeneity.

Each structure contains only objects belonging to the same conceptual category.

---

## Principle 2

Organization.

Every structure defines a precise organization of its constituent objects.

---

## Principle 3

Hierarchy.

Structures preserve the abstraction hierarchy established by the theoretical framework.

---

## Principle 4

Identity Preservation.

Objects maintain their mathematical identity independently of the structures to which they belong.

---

## Principle 5

Implementation Independence.

Mathematical structures remain valid independently of any computational realization.

---

# 10. Conclusion

The mathematical structures introduced in this document establish the formal organization of the mathematical objects constituting the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

The following document introduces the mathematical operations acting upon these structures and defines the formal mechanisms through which progressively richer mathematical representations are constructed.
