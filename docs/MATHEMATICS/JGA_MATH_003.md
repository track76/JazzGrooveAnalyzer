# JGA-MATH-003

# Fundamental Mathematical Definitions

**Document ID:** JGA-MATH-003

**Version:** 2.0

**Status:** Official

**Project:** Jazz Groove Analyzer (JGA)

**Author:** Angelo Tracanna

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish the fundamental mathematical definitions adopted by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

These definitions provide the conceptual basis upon which every mathematical object introduced by the project will subsequently be constructed.

No object is defined in this document.

Only the mathematical concepts required to define future objects are introduced.

---

# 2. Definition

A mathematical definition is a formal statement assigning an unambiguous meaning to a mathematical concept.

Within the Jazz Groove Analyzer, every mathematical definition satisfies the following conditions:

- it is unique;

- it is internally consistent;

- it is independent of implementation;

- it is compatible with the theoretical framework.

Once established, a definition remains valid throughout the entire project.

---

# 3. Mathematical Object

A mathematical object is an abstract entity introduced to formally represent a theoretical concept.

A mathematical object possesses mathematical existence independently of any computational implementation.

Its purpose is exclusively representational.

The computational realization of a mathematical object belongs to later stages of the project.

---

# 4. Mathematical Property

A mathematical property is a characteristic that remains true for a mathematical object independently of the computational method adopted.

Properties describe what an object is.

They never describe how an object is constructed.

---

# 5. Mathematical Structure

A mathematical structure is an organized collection of mathematical objects together with the formal rules governing their relationships.

Structures provide the mathematical organization of the framework.

They do not describe computational procedures.

---

# 6. Mathematical Transformation

A mathematical transformation is an abstract operation associating one mathematical object with another.

A transformation specifies the existence of a formal transition.

It does not specify the algorithm used to realize such transition.

---

# 7. Mathematical Relation

A mathematical relation is a formally defined association existing between two or more mathematical objects.

Relations describe dependencies.

They do not describe computational procedures.

---

# 8. Mathematical Model

A mathematical model is the coherent collection of mathematical definitions, objects, structures, transformations and relations describing a theoretical system.

Within the Jazz Groove Analyzer, the mathematical model constitutes the formal representation of the theoretical framework.

---

# 9. Fundamental Rules

Every mathematical definition introduced by the Jazz Groove Analyzer satisfies the following rules.

## Rule 1

Definitions precede objects.

---

## Rule 2

Objects precede structures.

---

## Rule 3

Structures precede transformations.

---

## Rule 4

Transformations precede computational algorithms.

---

## Rule 5

Algorithms never modify mathematical definitions.

---

# 10. Consistency Principle

Every mathematical definition shall remain compatible with:

- the theoretical foundations;

- the official glossary;

- previously established mathematical definitions.

No definition may contradict an earlier accepted definition.

---

# 11. Conclusion

The concepts introduced in this document constitute the elementary vocabulary of the mathematical model adopted by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

The following document will introduce the formal mathematical objects constituting the framework itself.
