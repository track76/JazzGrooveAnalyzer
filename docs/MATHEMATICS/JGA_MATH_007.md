# JGA-MATH-007

# Formal Mathematical Properties

**Document ID:** JGA-MATH-007

**Version:** 2.0

**Status:** Official

**Project:** Jazz Groove Analyzer (JGA)

**Author:** Angelo Tracanna

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish the formal mathematical properties governing the objects, structures and operations of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

These properties describe the intrinsic characteristics of the mathematical framework independently of any computational implementation.

They constitute the formal criteria that every future mathematical extension of the Jazz Groove Analyzer shall satisfy.

---

# 2. Mathematical Property

A mathematical property is a formally defined characteristic that remains invariant under every valid interpretation of the mathematical framework.

Properties describe the behavior of mathematical objects.

They do not describe computational procedures.

---

# 3. Identity

Every mathematical object possesses a unique mathematical identity.

The identity of an object is independent of:

- its computational representation;

- its temporal position;

- the operations applied to it.

Identity remains invariant throughout the entire mathematical framework.

---

# 4. Consistency

Every mathematical object, mathematical structure and mathematical operation shall remain consistent with all previously established definitions.

Consistency is preserved globally.

No local modification may invalidate the formal mathematical model.

---

# 5. Invariance

The mathematical meaning of every object remains invariant throughout the lifetime of the framework.

Future developments may introduce new objects.

Previously defined objects shall never change their mathematical meaning.

---

# 6. Hierarchy Preservation

Every valid mathematical construction preserves the abstraction hierarchy established by the theoretical framework.

No mathematical operation may bypass an intermediate abstraction level.

Every higher abstraction level depends upon the existence of the preceding level.

---

# 7. Semantic Preservation

Higher abstraction levels enrich the mathematical description.

They never invalidate lower abstraction levels.

Consequently,

Observation Objects remain mathematically valid after Representation Objects have been constructed.

The mathematical framework therefore preserves every level of abstraction simultaneously.

---

# 8. Determinacy

Every mathematical definition possesses one unique interpretation within the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

Ambiguous mathematical definitions are not permitted.

Whenever multiple interpretations are possible, the definition must be refined before becoming part of the mathematical framework.

---

# 9. Extensibility

The mathematical framework is extensible.

Future mathematical objects, structures and operations may be introduced.

Every extension shall satisfy the following conditions:

- compatibility with previous definitions;

- preservation of mathematical consistency;

- preservation of the abstraction hierarchy;

- preservation of implementation independence.

---

# 10. Fundamental Principles

Every mathematical property introduced by the Jazz Groove Analyzer satisfies the following principles.

## Principle 1

Identity Preservation.

Every object preserves its mathematical identity.

---

## Principle 2

Consistency Preservation.

Every extension preserves the consistency of the framework.

---

## Principle 3

Semantic Preservation.

Every abstraction enriches the mathematical description without replacing previous representations.

---

## Principle 4

Hierarchy Preservation.

Every mathematical construction respects the abstraction hierarchy.

---

## Principle 5

Long-Term Stability.

Previously established mathematical properties remain valid throughout the evolution of the project.

---

# 11. Conclusion

The mathematical properties introduced in this document establish the formal invariants governing the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

These invariants guarantee the coherence, stability and extensibility of the mathematical framework.
