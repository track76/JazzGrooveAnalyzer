# Formal Consistency Rules

**Document ID:** JGA-MATH-008

**Version:** 2.0

**Status:** Official

**Project:** Jazz Groove Analyzer (JGA)

**Author:** Angelo Tracanna

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish the formal consistency rules governing the complete mathematical framework of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

These rules define the constraints that every mathematical definition, object, structure, operation and future extension of the framework shall satisfy.

They guarantee the internal coherence of the mathematical model independently of its computational implementation.

---

# 2. Mathematical Consistency

Mathematical consistency is the property according to which every component of the framework is compatible with every other component.

Consistency is evaluated globally.

A mathematical definition is considered valid only if it does not contradict any previously accepted definition.

---

# 3. Rule of Uniqueness

Every theoretical concept shall correspond to exactly one mathematical object.

Likewise,

every mathematical object shall represent exactly one theoretical concept.

This correspondence is permanent throughout the entire framework.

---

# 4. Rule of Definition

Every mathematical object shall be formally defined before being used.

No mathematical operation may refer to undefined objects.

No mathematical structure may contain undefined entities.

Definitions always precede usage.

---

# 5. Rule of Hierarchy

Every mathematical object belongs to exactly one abstraction level.

Every mathematical operation preserves the abstraction hierarchy.

No mathematical construction may bypass intermediate abstraction levels.

The hierarchy established by the theoretical framework is immutable.

---

# 6. Rule of Semantic Consistency

Mathematical definitions shall preserve the semantic meaning established by the theoretical framework.

Mathematics formalizes theoretical concepts.

It never modifies them.

Whenever a mathematical formulation changes the meaning of a theoretical concept, the formulation shall be rejected.

---

# 7. Rule of Independence

The mathematical framework is completely independent of:

- programming languages;

- Digital Signal Processing algorithms;

- numerical methods;

- optimization procedures;

- machine learning techniques;

- software architectures.

Changes in computational implementation shall never modify the mathematical model.

---

# 8. Rule of Extension

Future extensions of the mathematical framework are permitted only if they satisfy all previously established definitions and consistency rules.

An extension shall never invalidate existing mathematical objects.

Extensions enrich the framework.

They never redefine it.

---

# 9. Rule of Stability

Once accepted, a mathematical definition remains stable.

Corrections may clarify existing definitions.

They shall never modify their mathematical meaning.

This guarantees the long-term stability of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

---

# 10. Rule of Traceability

Every computational component of the Jazz Groove Analyzer shall be traceable to a corresponding mathematical definition.

Likewise,

every mathematical definition shall be traceable to a theoretical concept established by the conceptual framework.

This guarantees complete traceability across the project.
