# Mathematical Operations

**Document ID:** JGA-MATH-006

**Version:** 2.0

**Status:** Official

**Project:** Jazz Groove Analyzer (JGA)

**Author:** Angelo Tracanna

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the mathematical operations adopted by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

Mathematical operations describe the formal mechanisms through which mathematical objects are combined, associated and transformed within the mathematical framework.

They are purely abstract entities and remain completely independent of any computational implementation.

---

# 2. Mathematical Operation

A mathematical operation is a formally defined rule acting upon one or more mathematical objects to produce another mathematical object or mathematical structure.

Operations describe mathematical behavior.

They do not describe computational procedures.

The existence of a mathematical operation does not imply the existence of an algorithm implementing it.

---

# 3. Unary Operations

A unary operation acts upon a single mathematical object.

Its result is another mathematical object belonging to the mathematical framework.

Unary operations preserve the internal consistency of the model.

---

# 4. Binary Operations

A binary operation acts upon two mathematical objects.

The resulting mathematical object depends exclusively on the formal definition of the operation.

Binary operations do not imply numerical calculations.

They describe abstract mathematical composition.

---

# 5. Composition of Operations

Several mathematical operations may be composed.

The result of one operation may constitute the input of another.

Operation composition allows the construction of progressively richer mathematical representations.

Composition never alters the mathematical validity of intermediate objects.

---

# 6. Closure

Every mathematical operation defined by the Jazz Groove Analyzer satisfies the principle of closure.

The result of every valid operation remains inside the mathematical framework of the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

No operation may generate entities external to the formal model.

---

# 7. Operation Consistency

Every mathematical operation satisfies the following conditions.

- mathematical validity;

- semantic coherence;

- compatibility with the abstraction hierarchy;

- implementation independence.

Operations shall never violate definitions established by previous documents.

---

# 8. Progressive Construction

Mathematical operations progressively enrich the formal description of musical time.

Lower abstraction levels remain mathematically valid after higher abstraction levels have been constructed.

Operations therefore extend the mathematical model without replacing existing objects.

---

# 9. Fundamental Principles

Every mathematical operation satisfies the following principles.

## Principle 1

Closure.

Every operation produces a result belonging to the mathematical framework.

---

## Principle 2

Consistency.

Operations preserve the coherence of the mathematical model.

---

## Principle 3

Abstraction.

Operations may increase the semantic level of representation but never reduce mathematical consistency.

---

## Principle 4

Composability.

Operations may be composed into larger mathematical processes.

---

## Principle 5

Implementation Independence.

Operations remain valid independently of any computational realization.

---
