# Mathematical Framework of the Jazz Groove Analyzer

**Document ID:** JGA-MATH-002

**Version:** 2.0

**Status:** Official

**Project:** Jazz Groove Analyzer (JGA)

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish the mathematical framework adopted by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

While the previous document defines the mathematical philosophy of the project, the present document establishes the formal environment in which every mathematical object of the JGA exists.

This framework provides the common mathematical foundation shared by all subsequent formal definitions.

---

# 2. Mathematical Framework

The mathematical framework of the Jazz Groove Analyzer is the collection of mathematical concepts, conventions and rules governing the formal representation of the project.

The framework does not describe computational procedures.

Instead, it defines the mathematical environment within which every object, relation and operation is formally represented.

---

# 3. Mathematical Domains

The Jazz Groove Analyzer distinguishes two conceptual mathematical domains.

## Observation Domain

The Observation Domain contains mathematical objects directly associated with observable acoustic phenomena.

Objects belonging to this domain describe measurable characteristics of the recorded audio signal.

No musical interpretation is assumed.

---

## Representation Domain

The Representation Domain contains mathematical objects representing progressively higher levels of musical abstraction.

Objects belonging to this domain are mathematical representations derived from previously established theoretical concepts.

They do not describe physical phenomena directly.

---

# 4. Mathematical Time

Every mathematical object defined by the Jazz Groove Analyzer exists within a common temporal framework.

Time constitutes the unique independent variable shared by both mathematical domains.

The mathematical framework itself does not prescribe how time is measured or sampled.

Such decisions belong exclusively to future implementation stages.

---

# 5. Mathematical Representation

Within the Jazz Groove Analyzer, every theoretical concept is represented by a corresponding mathematical entity.

Mathematical entities provide an abstract description of the theoretical model.

They are independent of programming languages, numerical methods and computational algorithms.

---

# 6. Mathematical Consistency

The mathematical framework satisfies the following consistency conditions.

- every theoretical concept possesses a unique mathematical representation;

- every mathematical representation corresponds to one theoretical concept;

- mathematical definitions remain valid independently of implementation;

- mathematical notation remains consistent throughout the project.

---

# 7. Separation of Concerns

The Jazz Groove Analyzer maintains a strict separation between four conceptual layers.

Theory

↓

Mathematics

↓

Algorithms

↓

Software

Each layer depends on the previous one.

No lower layer is allowed to modify the formal definitions established by a higher layer.

---

# 8. Framework Stability

The mathematical framework is considered implementation-independent.

Future improvements in:

- Digital Signal Processing;

- statistical methods;

- optimization techniques;

- machine learning;

- programming languages;

shall never modify the mathematical framework established by the present document.

Only the implementation may evolve.

---

# 9. Conclusion

The mathematical framework introduced in this document establishes the formal environment within which every mathematical entity of the Jazz Groove Analyzer is defined.
