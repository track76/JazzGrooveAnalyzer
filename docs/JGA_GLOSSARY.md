# Jazz Groove Analyzer (JGA)

# Official Glossary

Version 1.0

---

## Pulse Candidate

A Pulse Candidate is any detected acoustic event that may potentially belong to the ensemble pulse.

It represents a purely acoustic observation extracted from the audio signal and has no direct musical interpretation.

Pulse Candidates constitute the raw input used by the subsequent stages of the JGA analysis pipeline.

---

## Source Pulse Sequence

A Source Pulse Sequence is the ordered collection of Pulse Candidates generated by a single Metric Source.

Each sequence preserves the temporal evolution of one musical source and is analysed independently before being combined with the remaining sources.

---

## AudioStem

An AudioStem is a separated audio source produced by the source separation engine.

Each AudioStem represents an independent audio domain, such as Bass, Drums, Piano, Guitar, Vocals or Other instruments.

Every AudioStem is analysed independently by the Jazz Groove Analyzer before participating in the ensemble metric reconstruction.

Each AudioStem contains:

* stem identifier
* audio signal
* sample rate
* separation backend
* confidence score

---

## Metric Source

A Metric Source is a musical source that contributes consistently to the temporal organization of the ensemble.

The JGA identifies Metric Sources automatically by analysing their rhythmic persistence and periodic behaviour.

Typical Metric Sources include:

* Ride Cymbal
* Hi-Hat
* Brushes
* Double Bass
* Piano
* Guitar

Improvising instruments may temporarily cease to be Metric Sources without disappearing from the musical texture.

---

## Periodicity

Periodicity describes the persistence of a coherent rhythmic pulse over time.

It does not require perfectly identical temporal intervals.

Natural timing fluctuations produced by human performers are considered an intrinsic component of musical periodicity rather than measurement errors.

---

## Regularity

Regularity is the numerical measure of the temporal consistency between consecutive pulse intervals.

Regularity contributes to the estimation of Periodicity but does not define it completely.

A musical performance may present moderate temporal variations while preserving a strong sense of periodicity.

---

## Persistence

Persistence measures the duration during which a musical source maintains the same rhythmic behaviour.

Long persistence indicates that the source continuously supports the ensemble pulse.

---

## Continuity

Continuity describes the ability of a rhythmic behaviour to remain active without significant interruption.

Temporary silences or isolated missing events do not necessarily interrupt rhythmic continuity if the overall metric behaviour remains stable.

---

## Metric Compatibility

Metric Compatibility describes the ability of rhythmic variations to remain coherent with the underlying metric reference.

Intentional rhythmic subdivisions such as:

* Triplets
* Duplets
* Quintuplets
* Other intentional tuplets

are considered metrically compatible and therefore do not interrupt the metric reference reconstructed by the JGA.

These rhythmic figures are interpreted as legitimate subdivisions of the beat rather than timing deviations.

---

## Metric Role

The Metric Role represents the temporary musical function assumed by a Metric Source during the performance.

Typical roles include:

* Metric
* Non-Metric

The Metric Role is dynamic and may change continuously throughout the performance.

For example, a ride cymbal may carry the pulse during the theme, while becoming metrically irrelevant during a drum solo.

---

## Ensemble Metric Event (EME)

The Ensemble Metric Event (EME) is the elementary metric event reconstructed by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

An EME emerges from the temporal consensus of the active Metric Sources rather than from the performance of any individual musician.

It represents the smallest reconstructed metric unit used by the JGA to describe the collective pulse of the ensemble.

---

## Metric Cluster

A Metric Cluster is the ordered sequence of Ensemble Metric Events describing the evolution of the ensemble metric structure over time.

Metric Clusters constitute the fundamental temporal representation used by the JGA for groove analysis.

---

## Ensemble Metric Reference

The Ensemble Metric Reference is the final pulse estimated by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

It is reconstructed from the succession of Ensemble Metric Events generated by the active Metric Sources.

The Ensemble Metric Reference represents the collective pulse perceived at the ensemble level rather than the timing of any individual performer.

---

## Confidence

Confidence is the probability assigned by the Jazz Groove Analyzer to each recognised musical element.

Confidence values are associated with:

* separated audio sources
* recognised Metric Sources
* rhythmic components
* Ensemble Metric Events

Confidence allows the JGA to quantify the reliability of every stage of the analysis rather than treating every recognition as equally certain.

---

## BaseSeparator

BaseSeparator is the abstract interface implemented by every source separation backend used by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

The analysis pipeline is completely independent from the specific separation technology.

This design allows different separation engines to be adopted without modifying the rhythmic analysis algorithms.

---

## DemucsSeparator

DemucsSeparator is the current implementation of the BaseSeparator interface.

It uses HTDemucs to generate the AudioStems analysed by the Jazz Groove Analyzer.

Future versions of the JGA may replace or complement HTDemucs without affecting the remaining analysis pipeline.
