# F-025 — Analytical Problems

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document defines the class of scientific
problems that may be addressed by the Descriptor
Algebra.

The Descriptor Algebra is not an end in itself.

Its purpose is to provide mathematically rigorous
solutions to analytical questions arising from
observable musical behaviour.

## Scientific Principle

Every analytical computation shall correspond
to a well-defined scientific question.

No mathematical transformation shall exist
without an explicit analytical purpose.

## Analytical Workflow

Observable Behaviour

↓

BehaviourDescriptor

↓

DescriptorOperator

AnalyticalStructure

Scientific Answer

## Classes of Analytical Problems

The Descriptor Algebra may be used to study

- behavioural identity;

- behavioural similarity;

- behavioural evolution;

- behavioural stability;

- behavioural persistence;

- behavioural comparison;

- behavioural dependency.

Additional analytical problems may be introduced
provided they are formulated from observable
musical behaviour.

## Architectural Consistency

Analytical problems belong exclusively to the
Analytics Layer.

Representation does not formulate problems.

Quantification does not solve problems.

Analytics provides mathematically valid solutions.

## Consequences

Future Descriptor Operators shall be introduced
only when required to solve a formally defined
analytical problem.

This guarantees that the mathematical theory
remains driven by scientific observation rather
than by mathematical convenience.
