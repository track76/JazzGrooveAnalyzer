# BM-010 — Behaviour Transitions

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Transitions.

Behaviour Transitions describe the analytical
evolution from one Behaviour Regime to another.

## Scientific Principle

Observable musical behaviour evolves through
continuous or discontinuous transitions between
Behaviour Regimes.

Transitions are intrinsic properties of
Behaviour Space.

## Definition

Let

R₁

and

R₂

be two Behaviour Regimes.

A Behaviour Transition is the analytical process
through which observable behaviour moves from
R₁ to R₂.

## Input

Behaviour Regime

↓

Behaviour Evolution

## Transformation

Transition Analysis

## Output

Behaviour Transition

## Types of Transition

A transition may be

- continuous;

- discontinuous;

- stable;

- unstable.

The classification depends exclusively on
observable behaviour.

## Properties

Behaviour Transitions

- preserve Behaviour Space;

- connect Behaviour Regimes;

- are implementation independent;

- may be temporally localized.

## Architectural Consistency

Behaviour Transitions belong exclusively to
Behaviour Mathematics.

They never modify

- BehaviourDescriptors;

- DescriptorRelations;

- AnalyticalStructures.

## Scientific Consequences

Behaviour Transitions establish the mathematical
foundation for

- Behaviour Dynamics;

- Behaviour Evolution;

- Behaviour Topology;

- future Behaviour Geometry.
