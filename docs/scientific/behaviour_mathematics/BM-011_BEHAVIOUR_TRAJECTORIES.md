# BM-011 — Behaviour Trajectories

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Trajectories.

A Behaviour Trajectory represents the ordered
evolution of observable behaviour through
Behaviour Space over time.

## Scientific Principle

Observable musical behaviour is not a collection
of isolated states.

It is a continuous succession of behavioural
configurations.

Behaviour Trajectories describe this evolution.

## Definition

Let

B

be the Behaviour Space.

A Behaviour Trajectory is an ordered mapping

T : Time → B

associating every observable instant with a
Behaviour belonging to Behaviour Space.

## Input

Observable Behaviour

↓

Temporal Ordering

## Transformation

Trajectory Construction

## Output

Behaviour Trajectory

## Interpretation

A Behaviour Trajectory represents the analytical
path followed by an ensemble.

Trajectories are independent from performance
duration and implementation.

They describe behavioural evolution only.

## Properties

Behaviour Trajectories

- belong to Behaviour Space;

- preserve temporal ordering;

- may cross Behaviour Regimes;

- may contain Behaviour Transitions;

- are implementation independent.

## Architectural Consistency

Behaviour Trajectories belong exclusively to
Behaviour Mathematics.

They never modify

- BehaviourDescriptors;

- DescriptorRelations;

- AnalyticalStructures.

## Scientific Consequences

Behaviour Trajectories establish the mathematical
foundation for

- Behaviour Dynamics;

- Behaviour Geometry;

- Behaviour Topology;

- future comparative trajectory analysis.
