# BM-001 — Behaviour Functionals

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Functionals,
the first mathematical objects acting on
observable rhythmic behaviour.

Behaviour Functionals quantify global properties
of Behaviour Space without altering its
representation.

## Scientific Principle

A Behaviour Functional maps observable behaviour
into a mathematical quantity.

It does not represent behaviour.

It evaluates a property of behaviour.

## Definition

Let

B

be the Behaviour Space.

A Behaviour Functional is a mapping

F : B → ℝ

associating every behaviour with a real value.

## Input

Behaviour

## Transformation

Behaviour Functional

## Output

Real Number

## Interpretation

Behaviour Functionals measure properties of
behaviour rather than individual events.

The resulting value is a mathematical invariant
with respect to the selected analytical problem.

## Candidate Functionals

Future Behaviour Functionals may quantify

- stability;

- continuity;

- persistence;

- density;

- variability;

- symmetry;

- regularity.

The introduction of new Behaviour Functionals
shall always be justified by an observable
musical phenomenon.

## Architectural Consistency

Behaviour Functionals belong exclusively to
the Analytics Layer.

They operate after Behaviour Quantification
and after Descriptor Algebra.

They never modify BehaviourDescriptors.

They never modify Descriptor Relations.

## Scientific Consequences

Behaviour Functionals establish the bridge
between Descriptor Algebra and quantitative
mathematical analysis.
