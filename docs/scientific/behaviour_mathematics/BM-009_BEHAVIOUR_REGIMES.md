# BM-009 — Behaviour Regimes

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Regimes.

A Behaviour Regime represents a region of
Behaviour Space whose elements share the same
analytical behaviour.

## Scientific Principle

Observable musical behaviour evolves through
locally stable analytical regimes.

A Behaviour Regime is therefore an intrinsic
property of Behaviour Space rather than an
artifact of computation.

## Definition

Let

(B,d)

be the Behaviour Metric Space.

A Behaviour Regime is a subset

R ⊆ B

such that every behaviour contained in R
preserves the analytical properties defining
that regime.

## Input

Behaviour

↓

Behaviour Metric Space

↓

Behaviour Stability

## Transformation

Regime Identification

## Output

Behaviour Regime

## Interpretation

A Behaviour Regime groups behaviours that are
analytically equivalent with respect to the
selected analytical problem.

Behaviour Regimes do not imply identical
performances.

They identify common analytical organisation.

## Properties

Behaviour Regimes

- are defined on Behaviour Space;

- preserve Behaviour Stability;

- are implementation independent;

- may evolve over time.

## Architectural Consistency

Behaviour Regimes belong exclusively to
Behaviour Mathematics.

They never modify

- BehaviourDescriptors;

- DescriptorRelations;

- AnalyticalStructures.

## Scientific Consequences

Behaviour Regimes establish the mathematical
foundation for

- Behaviour Transitions;

- Behaviour Dynamics;

- Behaviour Topology;

- future Behaviour Geometry.
