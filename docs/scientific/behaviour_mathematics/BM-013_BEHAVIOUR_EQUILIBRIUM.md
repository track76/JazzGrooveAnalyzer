# BM-013 — Behaviour Equilibrium

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Equilibrium.

Behaviour Equilibrium represents a behaviour for
which no local analytical evolution is observed
under the current Behaviour Flow.

## Scientific Principle

Observable rhythmic behaviour may evolve towards
locally stable analytical configurations.

Such configurations are called Behaviour
Equilibria.

## Definition

Let

B

be the Behaviour Space.

Let

F

be the Behaviour Flow.

A behaviour

b ∈ B

is a Behaviour Equilibrium if the Behaviour Flow
at b does not induce further local analytical
evolution.

## Input

Behaviour

↓

Behaviour Flow

## Transformation

Equilibrium Evaluation

## Output

Behaviour Equilibrium

## Interpretation

Behaviour Equilibrium does not imply musical
immobility.

It indicates analytical stability with respect
to the current Behaviour Flow.

Different equilibria may exist simultaneously.

## Properties

Behaviour Equilibria

- belong to Behaviour Space;

- are invariant under local Behaviour Flow;

- are implementation independent;

- may be stable or unstable.

## Architectural Consistency

Behaviour Equilibria belong exclusively to
Behaviour Mathematics.

They never modify

- BehaviourDescriptors;

- DescriptorRelations;

- AnalyticalStructures.

## Scientific Consequences

Behaviour Equilibria establish the mathematical
foundation for

- Behaviour Attractors;

- Behaviour Geometry;

- Behaviour Dynamics;

- future analytical stability studies.
