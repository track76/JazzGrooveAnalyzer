# BM-002 — Behaviour Invariants

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Invariants.

Behaviour Invariants are mathematical properties
that remain unchanged under admissible analytical
transformations.

## Scientific Principle

An invariant represents an intrinsic property of
observable behaviour.

It is independent of the analytical path used to
derive it.

## Definition

Let

B

be the Behaviour Space.

Let

D

be the Descriptor Algebra.

A Behaviour Invariant is a property

I(B)

that remains unchanged under every admissible
Descriptor Operator preserving the analytical
context.

## Input

Behaviour

↓

Descriptor Algebra

## Transformation

Invariant Extraction

## Output

Behaviour Invariant

## Properties

A Behaviour Invariant shall be

- mathematically well-defined;

- reproducible;

- implementation independent;

- independent of operator ordering whenever
  guaranteed by the Descriptor Algebra;

- traceable to observable behaviour.

## Candidate Invariants

Future research may define invariants describing

- temporal persistence;

- structural stability;

- rhythmic symmetry;

- behavioural continuity;

- metric coherence.

The list is intentionally open.

## Architectural Consistency

Behaviour Invariants belong exclusively to the
Analytics Layer.

They never modify

- BehaviourDescriptors;

- DescriptorRelations;

- AnalyticalStructures.

They are analytical results derived from them.

## Scientific Consequences

Behaviour Invariants provide stable mathematical
references for comparing different performances,
ensembles and musical contexts.
