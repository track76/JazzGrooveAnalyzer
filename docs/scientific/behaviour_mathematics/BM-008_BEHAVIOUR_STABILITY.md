# BM-008 — Behaviour Stability

## Status

Scientific Foundation

## Purpose

This document introduces Behaviour Stability.

Behaviour Stability characterizes the persistence
of observable rhythmic behaviour under admissible
behavioural perturbations.

## Scientific Principle

A stable behaviour preserves its analytical
properties despite sufficiently small
behavioural variations.

Stability is evaluated within the Behaviour
Metric Space.

## Definition

Let

(B,d)

be the Behaviour Metric Space.

A behaviour

b ∈ B

is stable if every behaviour belonging to a
sufficiently small neighbourhood of b produces
analytical properties that remain within
admissible limits.

Behaviour

Stability Evaluation

## Output

Stability Property

## Interpretation

Behaviour Stability measures robustness rather
than similarity.

A stable behaviour is not necessarily identical
to neighbouring behaviours.

It preserves analytical consistency under
small perturbations.

Behaviour Stability

- depends on Behaviour Metric;

- depends on Behaviour Neighbourhoods;

- depends on Behaviour Continuity;

- is implementation independent.
